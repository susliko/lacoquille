## What it is

When a verb requires two objects — a person and a thing — French uses two pronouns. Unlike English ("give **it to me**"), French stacks them before the verb in a strict sequence: **MSSA-LL-YE**.

## The order sequence

**MSSA-LL-YE** (memorize this order):

| Position | Pronouns | Type | English |
|----------|----------|------|---------|
| 1 | me, te, se, nous, vous | Indirect object (person) | to me, to you, to himself |
| 2 | le, la, les | Direct object (thing) | it, them |
| 3 | lui, leur | Indirect object (person) | to him, to her, to them |
| 4 | y | Replace à + thing/place | to it, there |
| 5 | en | Replace de + thing/place | of it, from it, some |

**Memory aid:** *"**M**e **S**e **L**ui **Y** **En"* or imagine: **M**eet **S**omeone, **L**eave **Y**our **E**nvironment.

## Full combination table

| Combination | Example | English |
|-------------|---------|---------|
| me + le | *Il me **le** donne.* | He gives it to me. |
| me + la | *Elle me **la** montre.* | She shows it to me. |
| te + les | *Je te **les** offre.* | I offer them to you. |
| se + lui | *Il se **lui** présente.* | He introduces himself to him. |
| nous + le | *Elle nous **le** prête.* | She lends it to us. |
| vous + les | *Je vous **les** donne.* | I give them to you. |
| me + y | *Il me **y** envoie.* | He sends me there. |
| te + en | *Elle te **en** parle.* | She talks to you about it. |
| le + lui | *Je le **lui** offre.* | I offer it to him. |
| la + leur | *Elle la **leur** explique.* | She explains it to them. |
| les + y | *Il les **y** attend.* | He waits for them there. |
| le + en | *Je le **en** prive.* | I deprive him of it. |

## Special cases

### Verbs that always need two objects

Some verbs inherently require a person and thing:

| Verb | Example | Pronoun stack |
|------|---------|---------------|
| *donner* | Je donne le livre à Marie. | Je **le** lui donne. |
| *montrer* | Je montre la photo à Paul. | Je **la** lui montre. |
| *expliquer* | J'explique la règle à Marie. | Je **la** lui explique. |
| *dire* | Je dis la vérité à Pierre. | Je **la** lui dis. |
| *écrire* | J'écris une lettre à Marie. | Je **lui** écris. (no COD) |

### With compound tenses (passé composé)

Pronouns go between the auxiliary and past participle:

- *Je le lui ai donné.* — I gave it to him.
- *Elle nous les a montrés.* — She showed them to us.
- *Tu me l'as dit.* — You told me it.

### Negative sentences

Pronouns stay in position; *ne...pas* wraps the verb:

- *Je ne **le** lui donne **pas**.* — I don't give it to him.
- *Elle ne nous **les** a **pas** montré.* — She didn't show them to us.

## Common mistakes

**Wrong:** *Il y me le donne.* (y before other pronouns)
**Right:** *Il me le y donne.* ✗ still wrong — y en always LAST

**Correct order examples:**

| Wrong | Correct |
|-------|---------|
| *Il me y envoie.* | *Il m'y envoie.* (合并) |
| *Je le en prive.* | *Je l'en prive.* (合并) |
| *Elle leur y croit.* | *Elle y croit.* (lui/leur can't be with y) |

## Y + en restrictions

**Y** (à + thing/place) and **en** (de + thing) replace things or places, NOT persons.

- Replace *à Marie* → use **lui** (indirect) or **lui** alone (person)
- Replace *de Paris* → use **en** (place)
- Replace *de la musique* → use **en** (thing)

*Je pense à Paris.* → *J'**y** pense.*
*Je parle de Paris.* → *J'**en** parle.*
*Je parle à Marie.* → *Je **lui** parle.* (person → lui, not y)
