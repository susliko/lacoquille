## What it is

When a sentence requires two pronouns (one replacing the indirect object, one replacing the direct object, or one replacing a prepositional phrase), French orders them according to a fixed sequence. This sequence is different from English and must be memorized.

## The order sequence

```
1st position:  me, te, se, nous, vous      (reflexive/indirect)
2nd position:  le, la, les                  (direct object)
3rd position:  lui, leur                     (indirect object)
4th position:  y                             (à + noun)
5th position:  en                            (de + noun)
```

Memory aid: **MSSA-LL-YE** (Miss-uh-ELL-ee)

## Order table with examples

| Order | Combination | Example | Translation |
|-------|-------------|---------|-------------|
| me + le | me-le | Il **me le** donne. | He gives it to me. |
| te + la | te-la | Elle **te la** prête. | She lends it to you. |
| se + les | se-les | Il **se les** lave. | He washes them. |
| nous + le | nous-le | Elle **nous le** dit. | She tells it to us. |
| vous + la | vous-la | Je **vous la** donne. | I give it to you. |
| le + lui | le-lui | Je **le lui** donne. | I give it to him. |
| la + leur | la-leur | Elle **la leur** montre. | She shows it to them. |
| les + leur | les-leur | Il **les leur** offre. | He offers them to them. |
| le + y | le-y | Je **le y** mets. | I put it there. |
| la + en | la-en | Elle **la en** tire. | She pulls it from there. |
| en + le | en-le | J'**en le** sors. | I take it out from there. |

## Detailed rules

### First position: me/te/se/nous/vous

These pronouns can be reflexive (action returning to subject) or indirect objects. They always come first:

*Je me le lave.* — I wash it (for myself).
*Il te l'envoie.* — He sends it to you.
*Elle nous l'a donné.* — She gave it to us.

### Second position: le/la/les

Direct object pronouns come next (unless an indirect object pronoun from group 1 is present):

*Je le lui donne.* — I give it to him.
*Elle la leur montre.* — She shows it to them.

### Third position: lui/leur

True indirect objects (to him/her/them) come after direct objects:

*Je le donne à Paul.* → *Je **le lui** donne.* ✓
*Je donne Paul à Pierre.* (ungrammatical — Paul is not an indirect object here)

### Fourth position: y

After all object pronouns, **y** replaces à + noun:

*Je pense à Paris.* → *J'y pense.*
*Je le mets sur la table.* → *Je **le y** mets.* (unusual but grammatical)

### Fifth position: en

**En** always comes last, after y:

*Je viens de Paris.* → *J'en viens.*
*Je le prends de la table.* → *Je **le en** prends.* (rare but grammatical)

### Combined forms: contracted

When pronouns combine and one ends in a vowel while the next begins in a vowel, apostrophes connect them:

| Full form | Combined | Example |
|-----------|----------|---------|
| me + le | me-le → **me le** | Il me le donne. |
| te + le | te-le → **te le** | Il te le donne. |
| se + la | se-la → **se la** | Il se la lave. |
| le + y | le-y → **l'y** | Je l'y mets. |
| de + en | de-en → **d'en** | J'en parle. |

## Common pitfalls

### Incorrect order with lui/leur

English speakers often put the indirect object first (*him it gives*). French requires the direct object first:

Correct: *Je **le lui** donne.* — I give it to him.
Wrong: *Je **lui le** donne.* ✗

### Forgetting the contracted forms

*y + en* never contract with preceding pronouns, but vowel+vowel does:

*Je **le y** mets.* ✓ (le + y)
*Je **l'y** mets.* ✓ (le + y, contraction: le → l')
*Je **lui en** parle.* ✓ (lui + en)

### Agreement with past participle

When a direct object pronoun (le/la/les) precedes the auxiliary *avoir*, the past participle agrees:

*Je les leur ai données.* — I gave them to them. (les agrees: feminine plural)
*Il me l'a donné.* — He gave it to me. (no agreement: le is masculine singular)

But with **en**, no agreement even with quantities:
*J'en ai donné trois.* — I gave three (of them). (no agreement with en)
