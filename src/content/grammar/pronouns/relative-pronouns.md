## What it is

Relative pronouns introduce a clause that provides additional information about a noun. They "relate" the new clause back to the noun it describes. French has several relative pronouns depending on the grammatical role needed.

## Key forms

| Pronoun | Role | Translation |
|---------|------|-------------|
| qui | subject of verb in clause | who, which, that |
| que | direct object of verb in clause | who, which, that |
| dont | object of de | whose, of whom, from which |
| où | location/time | where, when |
| lequel | after prepositions | which (emphatic) |

## Usage rules

### Qui — subject pronoun

Use **qui** when the relative pronoun is the subject of the verb in the relative clause:

*Le chat qui dort = The cat that is sleeping*
(qui = le chat = subject of dormir)

*Les étudiants qui passent l'examen = The students who are taking the exam*
(qui = les étudiants = subject of passer)

### Que — direct object pronoun

Use **que** when the relative pronoun is the direct object of the verb in the relative clause:

*Le film que j'ai vu = The film (that) I saw*
(que = le film = direct object de voir)

*Les fleurs que tu as achetées = The flowers (that) you bought*
(que = les fleurs = direct object de acheter)

Note: **Que** becomes **qu'** before a vowel or mute h:
*La maison qu'il a construite* (not que il)

### Dont — de relationships

**Dont** replaces constructions with **de** (possession, origin, composition, description):

*La femme dont je connais le fils* = The woman whose son I know
(le fils de la femme → dont la femme → dont)

*L'ami dont je viens* = The friend from whom I'm coming
(je viens de l'ami → dont)

*Livre dont je parle* = The book I'm talking about
(je parle du livre → dont)

### Où — location and time

**Où** replaces locations and can express time:

*La ville où j'habite* = The city where I live
*Le pays où ils voyagent* = The country they're traveling to

Time expressions:
*Le jour où nous nous sommes rencontrés* = The day we met
*L'année où il est parti* = The year (when) he left

### Lequel / Laquelle / Lesquels / Lesquelles — after prepositions

Use these after prepositions when referring to things (not people):

*La table sur laquelle le livre repose* = The table the book rests on
*Les sujets auxquels nous avons parlé* = The subjects we talked about

Contracted forms after à: **auquel, auxquels, auxquelles**
Contracted forms after de: **duquel, desquels, desquelles**

With people, use **qui** (not lequel):
*La femme avec qui je parle* ✓
*La femme avec laquelle je parle* ✓ (also correct, more formal)

## Common pitfalls

### Confusion between qui and que

This is the most common error. Test: if you can replace the relative pronoun with "he/she/they" and the sentence still makes grammatical sense, it's the subject → use **qui**:

*L'homme qui parle* → He speaks. ✓ (qui = subject)
*L'homme que je vois* → I see him. ✓ (que = object)

### Dont vs De qui

For people, both are possible:
*L'homme dont je connais la femme* ✓
*L'homme de qui je connais la femme* ✓

For things, **dont** is standard:
*Le livre dont je parle* ✓ (not *de quoi je parle*)

### Que vs Ce que

*Que* refers to a specific noun: *Le livre que je lis* (the book that I read)
*Ce que* introduces a complete clause: *Ce que je lis est intéressant* (What I read is interesting)
