## What it is

Subject pronouns replace the noun that performs the action in a sentence. They tell us *who* or *what* is doing something. French has both singular and plural forms, plus a formal/informal distinction in the second person.

## Key forms

| Person | Singular | Plural |
|--------|----------|--------|
| 1st | je | nous |
| 2nd | tu | vous |
| 3rd | il (masc), elle (fem) | ils (masc/ mixed), elles (fem only) |
| 3rd generic | on | — |

## Usage rules

### Tu vs Vous

**Tu** is informal — use it with friends, family, children, peers, and anyone you've established a casual relationship with. **Vous** is formal or plural — use it with strangers, authority figures, elders, and when addressing more than one person.

*Tu es étudiant ?* (informal) — Are you a student?
*Vous êtes professeur ?* (formal) — Are you a teacher?
*Vous deux, venez ici.* (plural) — You two, come here.

### On vs Nous

**On** is the everyday "we" in spoken French. **Nous** is formal, literary, or required after prepositions like *chez* and *pour*.

In practice:
- Conversation: *On va au cinéma ce soir.* (We're going to the cinema tonight.)
- Formal writing: *Nous allons au cinéma ce soir.*
- After preposition: *Chez nous, on mange à 8h.* (At our place, we eat at 8.)

### Il vs Elle (and its)

**Il** and **elle** refer to specific nouns — people, animals, or things with grammatical gender. **Il** also serves as the "dummy subject" for expressions of time, weather, and general truths.

*Il pleut.* — It is raining.
*Il est trois heures.* — It is three o'clock.
*Ma voiture est rouge. Elle est belle.* — My car is red. It is beautiful.

### Ils vs Elles

**Ils** is the default masculine/mixed plural. **Elles** is used only when ALL referents are feminine.

*Ils sont partis.* (group of men, or mixed group)
*Elles sont parties.* (group of women only)

## Common pitfalls

### On replacing nous

In informal French, **on** essentially means "we" and takes third-person singular verb forms. This confuses English speakers who expect a plural verb. There's no choice here — **on** always conjugates as third person singular:

*On est contents.* (not *On sommes contents*) — We are happy.

### Formal vous vs plural vous

The word **vous** looks identical in both uses. Context makes the meaning clear:

*Vous êtes fatigué.* — Are you tired? (formal singular)
*Vous êtes fatigués.* (plural adjective agreement) — Are you all tired?

### Pronoun before the verb

In affirmative commands, the pronoun follows the verb with a hyphen:

*Parle-moi !* — Talk to me!

But in statements and questions, the pronoun comes before the verb:

*Je te parle.* — I am talking to you.
