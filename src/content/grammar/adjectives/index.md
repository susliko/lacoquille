## What adjectives do

Adjectives describe nouns — their color, size, shape, origin, and qualities. In French, they must agree with the noun in both **gender** and **number**.

## The three key rules

### 1. Agreement

Adjectives change form to match the noun:

| Form | Ending | Example |
|------|--------|---------|
| Masculine singular | — | *grand* |
| Feminine singular | **-e** | *grande* |
| Masculine plural | **-s** | *grands* |
| Feminine plural | **-es** | *grandes* |

- *un livre rouge* → *une table rouge**e***
- *des livres rouge**s*** → *des tables rouge**s***

### 2. Placement

Most adjectives go **after** the noun. A few common adjectives — BAGS (Beauty, Age, Goodness, Size) — go **before**.

| Position | Rule | Example |
|----------|------|---------|
| After noun | Default (origin, color, shape) | *une table ronde* |
| Before noun | BAGS + opinion adjectives | *un petit problème* |

### 3. Invariable

Some adjectives never change: compound colors (*bleu marine*), noun-turned-colors (*orange*, *marron*).
