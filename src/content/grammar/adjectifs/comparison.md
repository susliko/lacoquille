## What comparison is

Comparison expresses "more/less/as...as"; superlatives express "the most/least." Both work differently from English in French.

## Comparative: comparing two things

| French | English | Structure |
|--------|---------|-----------|
| plus...que | more...than | *plus grand que* |
| moins...que | less...than | *moins cher que* |
| aussi...que | as...as | *aussi bon que* |

- *Paris est **plus grand que** Lyon.* — Paris is bigger than Lyon.
- *Ce film est **moins long que** celui-là.* — This film is shorter than that one.
- *Marie est **aussi intelligente que** Pierre.* — Marie is as smart as Pierre.

## Superlative: the best/worst

Add **le/la/les** before the comparative:

| Form | Example |
|------|---------|
| le plus | *le plus grand* — the tallest |
| la plus | *la plus belle* — the most beautiful |
| les plus | *les plus petits* — the smallest |

- *C'est **le plus beau** film.* — It's the most beautiful film.
- *Elle est **la plus intelligente**.* — She is the most intelligent.
- *Ce sont **les plus grands**.* — They are the tallest.

After superlatives, use **de** (not *dans* or *à*):

- *Le plus grand bâtiment **de** Paris* — The tallest building in Paris

## Irregular forms

Three adjectives have **irregular comparative forms** you must memorize:

| Meaning | Comparative | Example |
|---------|-------------|---------|
| good | meilleur | *Ce vin est **meilleur** que celui-là.* |
| bad | pire / plus mauvais | *Cette idée est **pire**.* |
| well | mieux | *Il chante **mieux** que moi.* |

### Why not *plus bon*?

English "good → better → best" is regular. French *bon → meilleur* is **irregular** — you cannot say *plus bon*.

- *Ce restaurant est **meilleur** que l'autre.* ✓
- *Ce restaurant est **plus bon** que l'autre.* ✗

### Bien vs mieux

- *Il chante **bien**.* — He sings well.
- *Il chante **mieux** que toi.* — He sings better than you.
- *C'est **le mieux** que je peux faire.* — It's the best I can do.

### Pire vs plus mauvais

Both mean "worse," but **pire** is more informal:

- *C'est **pire** qu'avant.* — It's worse than before. (informal)
- *C'est **plus mauvais** qu'avant.* — It's worse than before. (standard)

## "More and more" / "Less and less"

Use **de plus en plus** (more and more) or **de moins en moins** (less and less) + adjective:

- *Le traffic est **de plus en plus** dense.* — Traffic is getting more and more dense.
- *Il parle **de moins en moins** français.* — He speaks less and less French.

Note: *de plus en plus* goes **before** the adjective:

- *de plus en plus **grand*** — taller and taller
- *de plus en plus **intéressant*** — more and more interesting

## Emphatic comparisons

For emphasis, add **bien** or **de loin**:

- *Il est **bien** plus intelligent qu'elle.* — He's much more intelligent than her.
- *C'est **de loin** le plus beau.* — It's by far the most beautiful.

## Quick reference

| Type | Structure | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|
| Comparative (more) | plus + adjective + que | *plus grand que* |
| Comparative (less) | moins + adjective + que | *moins cher que* |
| Comparative (as) | aussi + adjective + que | *aussi bon que* |
| Superlative | le/la/les plus + adjective | *le plus grand* |
| Irregular good | meilleur | *meilleur que* |
| Irregular well | mieux | *mieux que* |
| More and more | de plus en plus + adjective | *de plus en plus grand* |
