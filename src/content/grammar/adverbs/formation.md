## What it is

French forms most adverbs by combining an adjective with the suffix **-ment** (equivalent to English *-ly*). However, several special cases and irregular forms exist.

## Regular formation

Most adjectives simply add **-ment** to the feminine form:

| Masculine | Feminine | Adverb |
|-----------|----------|--------|
| rapide | rapide | rapidement |
| lent | lente | lentement |
| clair | claire | clairement |
| heureux | heureuse | happiest |

## Adjectives ending in -ant or -ent

These use **-amment** or **-emment**:

| Adjective | Adverb |
|-----------|--------|
| récent | récemment |
| évident | évidemment |
| puissant | puissamment |
| brillant | brillamment |

The **-ent** → **-emment** change preserves the soft sound.

## Irregular forms

These must be memorized:

| Adjective | Adverb |
|-----------|--------|
| bon | bien |
| mauvais | mal |
| ancien | anciennement |
| nouveau | nouvellement |
| gentil | gentiment |
|traître | traitreusement |

Note: *bon → bien* and *mauvais → mal* are the most important exceptions.

## adjectives ending in -e muet

Adjectives ending in a silent **-e** add **-ment** directly without using the feminine form:

| Adjective | Adverb |
|-----------|--------|
| vrai | vraiment |
| enorme | enormement |
| intime | intimement |

Compare with adjectives that change in the feminine:

| Adjective (masc) | Feminine | Adverb |
|------------------|----------|--------|
| clair | claire | clairement |
| lourd | lourde | lourdement |
