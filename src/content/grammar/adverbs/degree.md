## What it is

**Degree adverbs** express intensity, quantity, or comparison. They modify adjectives, other adverbs, or verbs to answer "how much?" or "to what extent?"

## Common degree adverbs

| Adverb | Meaning | Usage |
|--------|---------|-------|
| très | very | Intensifies: *très beau* — very beautiful |
| trop | too much | Excess: *trop cher* — too expensive |
| assez | enough / rather | Sufficient degree: *assez bon* — quite good |
| beaucoup | a lot | High quantity: *beaucoup manger* — to eat a lot |
| peu | little | Low degree: *peu probable* — unlikely |
| plus | more | Comparative: *plus grand* — bigger |
| moins | less | Comparative: *moins cher* — cheaper |
| autant | as much | Equal comparison: *autant que* — as much as |

## Comparison structures

French comparison uses specific patterns:

**Superiority:**
*Il est plus grand que moi.* — He is taller than me.
*Elle chante mieux que lui.* — She sings better than him.

**Inferiority:**
*Il est moins intelligent qu'elle.* — He is less intelligent than her.

**Equality:**
*Il est aussi grand que moi.* — He is as tall as me.
*Elle parle aussi bien que lui.* — She speaks as well as him.

## Adverbs with verbs

When modifying a verb, degree adverbs go after the verb:

*Je <v data-tense="present-indicatif">travaille</v> <v data-form="adverb">beaucoup</v>.* — I work a lot.
*Il <v data-tense="present-indicatif">dort</v> <v data-form="adverb">trop</v>.* — He sleeps too much.

## Special notes on comparison

- **Than** in comparisons is **que** (not comme for quality comparison).
- For equality of action, use **autant...que**: *Il travaille autant que moi.* — He works as much as me.
- *Plus* and *moins* can modify both adjectives (*plus grand*) and verbs (*je travaille plus*).

## Ne expletif interaction with degree

In complex sentences, the ne expletif can appear alongside degree expressions, but they are unrelated grammar points:

*Je crains qu'il ne soit plus là.* — I fear he may no longer be there. (*ne expletif*, not negation)
