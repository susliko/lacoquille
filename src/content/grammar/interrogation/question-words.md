## Basic question words

| Word | Meaning | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| Qui | Who | *Qui est la ?* — Who is there? |
| Que | What | *Que faites-vous ?* — What are you doing? |
| Ou | Where | *Ou habitez-vous ?* — Where do you live? |
| Quand | When | *Quand viens-tu ?* — When are you coming? |
| Comment | How | *Comment ca marche ?* — How does that work? |
| Pourquoi | Why | *Pourquoi pas ?* — Why not? |
| Combien | How many/much | *Combien ca coute ?* — How much is it? |

## Compound question words

Combine prepositions with question words for more specific questions:

| Structure | Example | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| A + qui | *A qui parles-tu ?* | To whom are you speaking? |
| De + quoi | *De quoi parles-tu ?* | What are you talking about? |
| Avec + quoi | *Avec quoi ecris-tu ?* | What are you writing with? |
| Pour + quoi | *Pour quoi faire ?* | For what purpose? |
| De + qui | *De qui parles-tu ?* | About whom are you talking? |

## Preposition placement

In informal French (intonation), the preposition can come at the end:

- *Tu parles a qui ?* — Who are you talking to?
- *Tu penses a quoi ?* — What are you thinking about?

In formal French (inversion), the preposition stays before the question word:

- *A qui parlez-vous ?* — To whom are you speaking?
- *De quoi parlez-vous ?* — What are you talking about?

With **est-ce que**, the preposition stays before the question word:

- *A qui est-ce que tu parles ?*
- *De quoi est-ce que tu parles ?*

## Special cases

### Que vs quoi

**Que** is used before consonants. Before vowels, it becomes **qu'**:

- *Qu'est-ce que tu veux ?* — What do you want?
- *Que faites-vous ?* — What are you doing?

**Quoi** is used after prepositions or standalone in informal speech:

- *Tu fais quoi ?* — What are you doing? (informal)
- *A quoi penses-tu ?* — What are you thinking about?

### Comment

**Comment** asks about manner or method:

- *Comment ca fonctionne ?* — How does that work?
- *Comment te sens-tu ?* — How do you feel?

To ask "What..." as in requesting information about something, use *qu'est-ce que*:
- *Qu'est-ce que tu fais ?* — What are you doing? (not *comment tu fais*)

### Combien

**Combien** needs **de** before a noun:

- *Combien de personnes ?* — How many people?
- *Combien de temps ?* — How long?
- *Combien ca coute ?* — How much does it cost?

## Word order with question words

### Direct questions (no inversion)

| Structure | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| Question word + verb | *Qui vient ?* — Who is coming? |
| Question word + subject + verb | *Quand arrive le train ?* — When does the train arrive? |
| Qu'est-ce que + subject + verb | *Qu'est-ce que tu veux ?* — What do you want? |
| Qu'est-ce qui + verb | *Qu'est-ce qui se passe ?* — What's happening? |

### Indirect questions (statement word order)

After verbs like *savoir*, *demander*, *voir*:

- *Je sais qui vient.* — I know who is coming.
- *Dis-moi ou tu habites.* — Tell me where you live.
- *Je ne sais pas ce que tu veux.* — I don't know what you want.
