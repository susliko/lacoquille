## The three methods

French has three ways to make a question. They all mean the same thing but differ in formality.

| Method | Example | Formality | Notes |
|--------|---------|-----------|-------|
| Intonation | *Tu viens ?* | Informal | Voice rises on the last syllable |
| Est-ce que | *Est-ce que tu viens ?* | Standard | Most common in spoken French |
| Inversion | *Viens-tu ?* | Formal | Subject and verb swap places |

## Intonation (informal)

Simply raise your voice at the end of the statement. The subject and verb order stays the same.

- *Tu pars ?* — Are you leaving?
- *Il fait froid ?* — Is it cold?
- *Vous voulez cafe ?* — Do you want coffee?

This is common in casual speech, with friends, and in quick exchanges.

## Est-ce que (standard)

Insert **est-ce que** after the subject. The statement word order stays intact.

- *Est-ce que tu viens ?* — Are you coming?
- *Est-ce que il fait beau ?* — Is the weather nice?
- *Est-ce que vous avez le temps ?* — Do you have time?

This is the workhorse of everyday French conversation. It's neutral and natural.

## Inversion (formal)

Swap the subject and verb. The verb comes first.

- *Viens-tu ?* — Are you coming?
- *Parlez-vous francais ?* — Do you speak French?
- *Ou habitez-vous ?* — Where do you live?

Use this in formal situations: presentations, ceremonies, or very formal writing.

## Inversion details

### Pronoun subjects

With je: invert to *parle-je* ? — but this is rare and sounds archaic. Use *est-ce que je...* instead.

With il/elle/on: if the verb ends in a vowel, add **-t-**:
- *Va-t-il ?* — Is he going?
- *Parle-t-elle ?* — Is she speaking?
- *On-t-il ?* — do they have? (less common)

### Noun subjects

Keep the noun first, then invert the pronoun:
- *Pierre joue-t-il ?* — Is Pierre playing?
- *Marie vient-elle ?* — Is Marie coming?

Never invert a noun subject directly: *Joue Pierre ?* is wrong.

## Choosing the right method

| Situation | Method |
|-----------|--------|
| Talking to friends | Intonation |
| Conversation, work, school | Est-ce que |
| Formal writing, speeches | Inversion |

In practice, **est-ce que** covers most situations. Use it unless you have a reason to sound either very casual (intonation) or very formal (inversion).
