## What it is

In French, most adjectives follow the noun they modify, but a small but important group of adjectives — mostly about **opinion, size, age, and beauty** — go **before** the noun. Position can also change meaning.

## After the noun: the default

Most adjectives go **after** the noun. These typically describe objective qualities:

| Category | Example | Meaning |
|----------|---------|---------|
| Size | *grand* | big |
| Age | *ancien* | ancient, old |
| Shape | *rond* | round |
| Color | *rouge* | red |
| Origin | *français* | French |
| Material | *métallique* | metallic |
| Purpose | *médical* | medical |

- *une maison **ancienne*** — an old house
- *une table **ronde*** — a round table
- *unvetement **bleu*** — a blue garment

## Before the noun: the BAGS group

A set of common adjectives — organized as **BAGS** — usually appear **before** the noun:

| | French | Example |
|--|--------|---------|
| **B**eauty | *beau, belle, joli* | *un bel homme* |
| **A**ge | *vieux, nouvelle, jeune* | *une jeune fille* |
| **G**oodness | *bon, mauvais* | *un bon restaurant* |
| **S**ize | *grand, petit* | *un petit problème* |

Other "opinion" adjectives also go before:
- *un **gentil** enfant* — a nice child
- *une **jolie** femme* — a pretty woman
- *le **dernier** jour* — the last day
- *le **seul** problème* — the only problem

## Meaning changes with position

Some adjectives change meaning depending on placement:

| Adjective | Before noun | After noun |
|-----------|-------------|------------|
| *grand* | important, great | tall |
| *cher* | dear | expensive |
| *mauvais* | wicked, bad | bad at (skill) |
| *propre* | own | clean |
| *dernier* | latest, final | last (in series) |
| *seul* | only | alone |
| *petit* | minor, petty | small, short |

- *un **grand** homme* — a great man ( Napoleon, Churchill)
- *un homme **grand*** — a tall man (physically)
- *mon **propre** livre* — my own book
- *un livre **propre*** — a clean book
- *la **dernière** semaine* — the final week (of a series)
- *la semaine **dernière*** — last week (the week before this one)

## Multiple adjectives

When a noun has both pre-noun and post-noun adjectives:

**Before + after:** *un **petit** jardin ** carré** — a small square garden

**Two before:** Usually combined: *un **vieux** petit** ami** — an old friend (both before)

**Two after:** Joined by *et*: *une robe **verte et bleue*** — a green and blue dress

## The nuance rule

When in doubt, try both positions:

- *un **petit** problème* — a small problem (troublesome)
- *un problème **petit*** — a minor problem (less serious)

Post-noun position often feels more descriptive/literal; pre-noun often feels more subjective or emphatic.
