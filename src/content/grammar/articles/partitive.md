## What it is

Partitive articles express **an unspecified quantity** of something uncountable. They answer "some?" rather than "a?" or "how many?" Use them with mass nouns (water, bread, sugar) and abstract concepts (patience, courage).

## Forms

| Before | Form | Example |
|--------|------|---------|
| Consonant (masculine) | du | *du pain* — some bread |
| Consonant (feminine) | de la | *de la musique* — some music |
| Vowel/h mute (any gender) | de l' | *de l'eau* — some water |
| Plural (rarely used) | des | *des soins* — some care |

## du, de la, de l' — what's the difference?

**du** = de + le (masculine noun starting with consonant)
**de la** = feminine noun starting with consonant
**de l'** = any gender before vowel or mute h

- *du café* — some coffee (masculine)
- *de la confiture* — some jam (feminine)
- *de l'air* — some air (masculine starting with vowel)
- *de l'heure* — some time (feminine starting with h)

## Partitive vs Indefinite

| | Partitive | Indefinite |
|---|---|---|
| Noun type | Uncountable (mass nouns) | Countable |
| English | some | a, an, some |
| Example | *du pain* (bread) | *un pain* (a loaf of bread) |

Think of it this way:
- *du pain* = some bread (you don't know how much)
- *un pain* = a loaf of bread (a countable unit)

## With quantity expressions

Partitive articles combine naturally with quantity words:

- *beaucoup **de*** — a lot of: *beaucoup de travail* — a lot of work
- *peu **de*** — little/few: *peu de chances* — few chances
- *un peu **de*** — a little: *un peu de sel* — a little salt
- *trop **de*** — too much: *trop de bruit* — too much noise
- *assez **de*** — enough: *assez de temps* — enough time
- *la moitié **de*** — half: *la moitié du temps* — half the time

## De instead of partitive after negation

Like indefinite articles, partitive articles become **de** after negation:

| Affirmative | Negative |
|-------------|----------|
| J'ai *du* pain. | Je n'ai pas *de* pain. |
| Elle veut *de la* musique. | Elle ne veut pas *de* musique. |
| Nous avons *de l'*argent. | Nous n'avons pas *d'argent*. |

## The deceptive des

Remember: **des** looks identical whether it's indefinite plural or partitive plural. The noun type tells you which it is:

- *des livres* — some books (countable → indefinite)
- *des soins* — some care (uncountable → partitive)

Context usually makes the meaning clear.

## Common pitfalls

- Don't use partitive with countable singular nouns: *un livre* (a book), not *du livre*
- The negation rule catches many learners: always change partitive to *de* after *pas*, *plus*, *jamais*, etc.
- *avoir besoin de* takes **de** (not partitive): *J'ai besoin de repos* — I need some rest.
