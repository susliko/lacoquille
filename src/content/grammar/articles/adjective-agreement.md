## What it is

In French, adjectives must match the noun they describe — not just in number (like English "big/bigs") but also in **gender**. A masculine adjective looks different from its feminine counterpart.

## Regular agreement

Most adjectives add **-e** for feminine and **-s/-es** for plural:

| Base (masculine) | Feminine | Masculine plural | Feminine plural |
|-------------------|----------|------------------|-----------------|
| grand | grande | grands | grandes |
| petit | petite | petits | petites |
| rouge | rouge | rouges | rouges |

- *un livre **rouge*** — a red book (masculine)
- *une table **roug****e*** — a red table (feminine)
- *des livres **rouges*** — red books (masculine plural)
- *des tables **rouges*** — red tables (feminine plural)

Note: If the masculine form already ends in -e, the feminine doesn't change: *un livre rouge* → *une table rouge*.

## Irregular agreements

Some common adjectives have irregular feminine or plural forms:

| Masculine | Feminine | Meaning |
|-----------|----------|---------|
| bon | bonne | good |
| nouveau | nouvelle | new |
| vieux | vieille | old |
| beau | belle | beautiful |
| blanc | blanche | white |
| sec | sèche | dry |
| frais | fraîche | fresh |
| public | publique | public |

- *un **bon** restaurant* → *une **bonne** réponse*
- *un **nouveau** travail* → *une **nouvelle** idée*

## Special forms before vowels

Five adjectives have a **third form** for when they come before a singular vowel sound. This "befriended" form adds -l, -t, or -v before the vowel:

| Base | Before consonant | Before vowel |
|------|------------------|--------------|
| beau | beau | bel |
| nouveau | nouveau | nouvel |
| vieux | vieux | vieil |
| fou | fou | fol |
| mou | mou | mol |

- *un **beau** livre* — a beautiful book
- *un **bel** homme* — a handsome man
- *une **belle** femme* — a beautiful woman
- *un **nouvel** an* — a new year
- *un **viel** homme* — an old man (before vowel)

## Invariable adjectives

Some adjectives never change form:

### Compound colors
Colors made from two words are invariable:

| Invariable | From |
|------------|------|
| bleu marine | navy blue |
| rouge bordeaux | burgundy |
| bleu ciel | sky blue |
| vert olive | olive green |

- *des robes **bleu marine*** — navy blue dresses

### Noun-turned-colors
Colors that come from nouns stay invariable:

- *des pulls **orange*** — orange sweaters (orange = the fruit/noun)
- *des murs **marron*** — brown walls (marron = the chestnut)

But: *une robe **rose*** (rose is also an adjective)

### Other invariable cases
- *une situation **extrême*** — an extreme situation
- *das ** weekend ** — English borrowings often stay invariable

## Common pitfalls

- Don't apply masculine forms to feminine nouns: *une voiture **vert*** ✗ → *une voiture **verte*** ✓
- Remember the special forms before vowels: *le bel exemples* ✗ → *le bel exemple* ✓
- Learn adjective genders alongside vocabulary — guessing leads to errors
- When two adjectives share a noun, each agrees: *une robe petit**e** et rouge***e***
