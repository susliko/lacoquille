## What it is

Indefinite articles introduce something not yet known to the listener, or speak about things in a general sense. **un** (masculine), **une** (feminine), and **des** (plural) correspond to "a," "an," and "some" (or just the plural form).

## Forms

| Gender | Singular | Plural |
|--------|----------|--------|
| Masculine | un | des |
| Feminine | une | des |

- *un livre* — a book
- *une fleur* — a flower
- *des enfants* — some children

## Un vs une: the gender reveal

The choice between *un* and *une* matters — it signals the gender of the noun:

- *un lampe* ✗ → *une lampe* (feminine)
- *une livre* ✗ → *un livre* (masculine)

When in doubt, learn the article with the noun.

## The partitive confusion: des vs du/de la/de l'

Here's where it gets tricky. **des** looks exactly like the plural of indefinite articles, but it can also be partitive (meaning "some"). The difference:

- **Indefinite des** → refers to countable things: *des livres* (some books, a few books)
- **Partitive du/de la/de l'** → refers to uncountable things: *du pain* (some bread)

The context and noun type determine which it is:

- *J'ai **des idées**.* — I have some ideas. (countable → indefinite)
- *J'ai **du pain**.* — I have some bread. (uncountable → partitive)

## De vs des after negation

This is a critical rule: after negation (*ne...pas*, *ne...plus*, *ne...jamais*), **des** changes to **de**:

| Affirmative | Negative |
|-------------|----------|
| J'ai *des* frères. | Je n'ai pas *de* frères. |
| Elle a *des* problèmes. | Elle n'a pas *de* problèmes. |
| Nous avons *des* amis. | Nous n'avons pas *d'amis*. |

- *J'ai des enfants.* → *Je n'ai pas **d'enfants**.* ✓
- *Je n'ai pas des enfants.* ✗

Note: With *ne...aucun*, you also use **de**: *Je n'ai **aucun** doute.*

## Des + adjective rule

When **des** precedes an adjective before a noun, it changes to **de**:

- *des grandes maisons* — big houses (not *des grandes maisons* with *des*)
- *des petits chats* — small cats

But if the adjective follows the noun, **des** stays:

- *des maisons grandes* ✗ (wrong structure)

## Common pitfalls

- Don't use *un/une* with uncountable nouns: *J'ai un eau* ✗ → *J'ai de l'eau* ✓
- Remember the negation rule: *de* not *des* after *pas*, *plus*, *jamais*, *rien*, *aucun*
- *Des* can mean "some" or nothing in English: *J'ai des livres* = "I have books" or "I have some books" — both translations work.
