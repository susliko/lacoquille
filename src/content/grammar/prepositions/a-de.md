## The contractions

When **à** or **de** meets a definite article, they merge:

| Contraction | Full form | Meaning |
|-------------|-----------|---------|
| **au** | à + le | to the / at the |
| **aux** | à + les | to the / at the (plural) |
| **du** | de + le | of the / from the |
| **des** | de + les | of the / from the (plural) |

## With places and countries

### Countries with à and de

| Country | With à (to) | With de (from) |
|---------|-------------|----------------|
| France (f.) | en France | de France |
| Canada (m.) | au Canada | du Canada |
| États-Unis (m.pl.) | aux États-Unis | des États-Unis |
| Japon (m.) | au Japon | du Japon |
| Belgique (f.) | en Belgique | de Belgique |

### The en exception

With feminine countries, use **en** (not au/aux) for to/from:
- *Je vais **en France**.* — I'm going to France.
- *Je viens **de France**.* — I come from France.

Masculine countries use au/aux and du/des:
- *Je vais **au Canada**.* — I'm going to Canada.
- *Je viens **du Canada**.* — I come from Canada.

## De + les = des (with exceptions)

### After negation

After **ne...pas** or other negation words, *des* often becomes *de*:

- *J'ai des enfants.* → *Je n'ai pas **de** enfants.* (I have children → I don't have children)
- *Elle a des problèmes.* → *Elle n'a pas **de** problèmes.* (She has problems → She doesn't have problems)

### In expressions of quantity

With expressions like *beaucoup de*, *peu de*, *trop de*:

- *J'ai **beaucoup de** travail.* — I have a lot of work.
- *Il y a **peu de** gens.* — There are few people.

## à vs de with verbs

This is where it gets tricky. Some verbs take **à**, others take **de**, and it rarely matches English:

| Verb | Preposition | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **penser à** | à | *Je pense à toi.* — I'm thinking of you. |
| **croire à** | à | *Je crois en Dieu.* — I believe in God. |
| **réver de** | de | *Je rêve d'elle.* — I dream of her. |
| **aller à** | à | *Je vais à Paris.* — I'm going to Paris. |
| **venir de** | de | *Je viens de Lyon.* — I come from Lyon. |
| **manquer de** | de | *Il manque de patience.* — He's lacking patience. |

See the [verb combinations page](/reference/grammar/prepositions/verb-combinations) for more.

## Common pitfalls

- **Never** write *à le* or *de le* — always contract first.
- **en** replaces *à + les* (aux) and *de + les* (des) only for feminine countries.
- Watch for **de** vs **des** after negation — this is one of the most common errors.
