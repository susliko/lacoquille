## What it is

**Sortir** expresses motion **out of** an enclosed space. It emphasizes exiting through a boundary — going from inside to outside a container, room, building, or any defined space. The origin is specified with **de**.

## Motion semantics

*Sortir* encodes **exiting** from an enclosure:

- *Je sors de la maison* — I'm going out of the house
- *Le livre sort de la boîte* — The book comes out of the box

The preposition **de** indicates the origin: *sortir de* means "to go out of."

This contrasts with:
- *entrer* — to go into (opposite direction)
- *partir* — to leave from (emphasizes departure, not the act of exiting through something)

Think of *sortir* as emphasizing the **physical crossing of a boundary** from inside to outside.

## *Être en train de* + infinitive

*Sortir* works naturally with this periphrasis:

- *Je suis en train de sortir* — I'm in the process of going out

## *Venir de* + infinitive

*Sortir* combines naturally with *venir de*:

- *Je viens de sortir* — I've just gone out
- *Nous venons de sortir du cinéma* — We've just come out of the cinema

## Common expressions & idioms

- **Sortir de** — to go out of, to leave *Sortir de la ville*
- **Sortir avec** — to go out with, to date *Je sors avec mes amis*
- **Sortir de** (figurative) — to be beyond *Ça sort de la question*
- **Mettre dehors / Sortir** — to throw out, to evict
- **En sortir** — to get out of it, to come out of it *Je ne sais pas comment en sortir*
