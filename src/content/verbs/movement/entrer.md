## What it is

**Entrer** expresses motion **into** an enclosed space. The key element is crossing a threshold — going from outside to inside a container, room, building, or any defined space. The destination is specified with **dans** or **en**.

## Motion semantics

*Entrer* answers the question "Where to?" and implies **crossing a boundary into an enclosure**:

- *J'entre dans la maison* — I go into the house
- *Entrez !* — Enter! / Come in!
- *Le câble entre dans le mur* — The cable goes into the wall

The preposition **dans** typically accompanies *entrer* when specifying a destination. The motion implies that the space was previously outside the enclosure.

This contrasts with *sortir*, which describes motion **out of** an enclosure.

## *Être en train de* + infinitive

*Entrer* works naturally with this periphrasis:

- *Je suis en train d'entrer* — I'm in the process of entering

## *Venir de* + infinitive

*Entrer* combines naturally with *venir de*:

- *Je viens d'entrer* — I've just entered
- *Elle vient d'entrer dans la salle* — She just entered the hall

## Common expressions & idioms

- **Entrer dans** — to enter, to go into *Entrer dans un magasin*
- **Entrer en** — to enter into, to begin to be *Entrer en contact, Entrer en guerre*
- **Faire entrer** — to show in, to let in *Faites-les entrer !*
- **Entrer par** — to enter through *Entrez par la porte de derrière*
- **Y entrer** — to fit in, to go in there *Ces livres n'entrent pas dans le sac*
