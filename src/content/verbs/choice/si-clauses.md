French conditional sentences follow strict tense-pairing rules. Knowing the three canonical patterns covers 95% of usage.

## The golden rule

> **Never use conditionnel in the si-clause.** Ever. The si-clause always takes indicatif or plus-que-parfait.

## The three patterns

### Pattern 1 — Real / open condition (présent or future possibility)

| Si-clause | Result clause |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Présent indicatif** | **Futur simple** (or présent, or impératif) |

*Si tu **viens**, je serai content.* — If you come, I'll be happy.
*Si tu **as** faim, mange quelque chose.* — If you're hungry, eat something.

The condition is real or plausible. "If X (and it might happen), then Y will follow."

---

### Pattern 2 — Hypothetical / contrary-to-present

| Si-clause | Result clause |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Imparfait** | **Conditionnel présent** |

*Si j'**avais** le temps, je **voyagerais** plus.* — If I had time (but I don't), I would travel more.
*Si elle **était** là, tout **irait** mieux.* — If she were here (but she's not), everything would be better.

The condition is presented as hypothetical or contrary to present reality.

---

### Pattern 3 — Unrealised past / regret

| Si-clause | Result clause |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Plus-que-parfait** | **Conditionnel passé** |

*Si j'**avais su**, je **serais venu**.* — If I had known (but I didn't), I would have come.
*Si elle **avait travaillé**, elle **aurait réussi**.* — If she had worked (but she didn't), she would have succeeded.

The condition is about the past and cannot be fulfilled — pure hypothetical or regret.

---

## Mixed conditionals

It's possible to mix pattern 2 and 3 when the condition is past but the consequence is present:

*Si tu **avais étudié** (P3 in si), tu **comprendrais** maintenant (P2 in main).* — If you had studied (back then), you would understand (now).

---

## Other uses of si

*Si* doesn't always introduce a conditional. It also expresses:

- **Indirect question** (with indicatif): *Je me demande si elle **viendra**.* — I wonder if she will come.
- **Suggestion / polite proposal**: *Si on **allait** au cinéma ?* — What if we went to the cinema? (imparfait alone, no result clause)
- **Exclamation / wish**: *Si seulement il **était** là !* — If only he were here!

## Cheat sheet

| Condition type | Si-clause | Main clause |
|----------------|-----------|-------------|
| Real / open | présent | futur / présent / impératif |
| Hypothetical (present) | imparfait | conditionnel présent |
| Unrealised (past) | plus-que-parfait | conditionnel passé |
| Mixed | plus-que-parfait | conditionnel présent |
