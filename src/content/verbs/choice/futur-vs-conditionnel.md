Both tenses share the same stem — the difference is entirely in the endings and in the meaning they encode.

## Same stem, different endings

| | Stem | Endings |
|---|---|---|
| **Futur** | infinitive (drop -e for -re) | -ai, -as, -a, -ons, -ez, -ont |
| **Conditionnel** | same stem | -ais, -ais, -ait, -ions, -iez, -aient |

*parler →* **parlerai** (futur) vs **parlerais** (conditionnel)
*être →* **serai** vs **serais**

The conditionnel endings are the same as imparfait endings — a useful memory hook.

## When to use each

### Futur simple — "will"

Use futur for actions that **will** happen:

- Scheduled future events: *Le train **partira** à 18h.*
- Confident predictions: *Il **fera** beau demain.*
- Future consequences of a real condition (*si + présent*): *Si tu viens, je **serai** là.*
- After time conjunctions referring to future (*quand, dès que, lorsque*): *Quand elle **arrivera**, appelez-moi.*

### Conditionnel présent — "would"

Use conditionnel for actions that **would** happen under a condition or as a hypothesis:

- Hypothetical result (*si + imparfait*): *Si j'avais le temps, je **lirais** plus.*
- Polite requests: ***Pourriez**-vous m'aider ?* / *Je **voudrais** un café.*
- Wishes and suggestions: *On **pourrait** aller au parc.*
- Future from a past perspective (reported speech): *Il a dit qu'il **viendrait**.*
- Unverified information (**journalistic conditional**): *Le suspect **serait** en fuite.* — The suspect is allegedly on the run.

## The journalistic conditional

This is one of the most important uses of the conditionnel in real French:

> The journalist uses conditionnel to signal that the information is unconfirmed — they are reporting a claim, not stating a fact.

*La société **aurait** licencié 200 employés.* = "The company is reported to have laid off 200 employees."
*Il **serait** impliqué dans l'affaire.* = "He is allegedly involved in the case."

You will see this constantly in French news. The conditionnel is the marker of epistemic distance — "I'm not vouching for this."

## Key contrasts

| Futur | Conditionnel | Distinction |
|-------|-------------|-------------|
| *Il **viendra**.* | *Il **viendrait**.* | will come vs would come |
| *Quand tu **arriveras**…* | — (can't use conditionnel after *quand* for future) | time conjunction rule |
| *Si tu viens, il **sera** là.* | *Si tu venais, il **serait** là.* | real vs hypothetical condition |
| *Ce **sera** difficile.* | *Ce **serait** difficile.* | confident prediction vs hedged opinion |

## Compound forms

The same distinction applies to the compound forms:
- **Futur antérieur** (*aura fini*) = will have done
- **Conditionnel passé** (*aurait fini*) = would have done
