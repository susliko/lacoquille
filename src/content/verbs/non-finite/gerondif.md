## What is the gérondif

The **gérondif** is formed with the preposition **en** + the **participe présent** (present participle). It is used to express an action that occurs **simultaneously** with the main verb, providing circumstances such as manner, cause, or condition.

*Il parle <v data-tense="gerondif">en mangeant</v>.* — He talks while eating.

The gérondif is always **invariable** — the -ant ending never changes, regardless of the subject's gender or number.

## How to form

Take the **nous** form of the present indicative, drop **-ons**, and add **-ant**:

| Infinitive | *Nous* form | Gérondif |
|------------|-------------|----------|
| *parler* | parlons | **en parlant** |
| *finir* | finissons | **en finissant** |
| *vendre* | vendons | **en vendant** |
| *être* | sommes | **en étant** |
| *avoir* | avons | **en ayant** |

**Irregular gérondifs:**

| Verb | Gérondif |
|------|----------|
| *savoir* | en sachant |
| *pouvoir* | en pouvant |
| *vouloir* | en voulant |
| *devoir* | en devant |

## When to use

### Simultaneous action ("while," "by")

The gérondif's primary function is to show that two actions happen at the same time:

*Je lisais <v data-tense="gerondif">en mangeant</v>.* — I was reading while eating.
*Elle est tombée <v data-tense="gerondif">en descendant</v> les escaliers.* — She fell while going down the stairs.

### Manner ("by")

When the gérondif explains *how* something is done:

*On apprend <v data-tense="gerondif">en lisant</v>.* — We learn by reading.
*Il a réussi <v data-tense="gerondif">en travaillant</v> dur.* — He succeeded by working hard.

### Cause ("because of," "on account of")

*<v data-tense="gerondif">En fumant</v>, il a abîmé ses poumons.* — By smoking, he damaged his lungs.
*<v data-tense="gerondif">En arrivant</v> tard, il a raté le début.* — By arriving late, he missed the beginning.

### Condition ("if," "when")

*<v data-tense="gerondif">En lavorant</v> plus, tu réussirais.* — By working more, you would succeed. (= if you worked more)

## Gérondif vs pour + infinitive (purpose)

| Construction | Meaning | Example |
|--------------|---------|---------|
| **gérondif** (*en* + -ant) | simultaneous action, manner, cause | *Il a réussi en essayant* — He succeeded by trying |
| **pour** + infinitive | purpose, intent (future-oriented) | *Il a réussi pour gagner* — He succeeded to win (in order to win) |

*J'aiaprouvé <v data-tense="gerondif">en lisant</v>* — I improved by reading (ongoing circumstance).
*J'aiaprouvé <v data-tense="infinitif">pour lire</v> mieux* — I studied to read better (purpose).

## Common pitfalls

- **Always invariable** — never write *en parlante*, even if the subject is feminine. The -ant ending never takes agreement markers.
- **Same subject required** — the gérondif action must share the same subject as the main verb. To express "while I was reading, she arrived," you cannot use a gérondif for one clause and a finite verb for the other — you need *pendant que* or a participial phrase.
- **Don't confuse with the participe présent** used after other prepositions — *en marchant* = gérondif, but *pour marchant* (with a different preposition) would be unusual. The gérondif specifically requires *en*.
