## What is the participe présent

The **participe présent** is a non-finite verb form that expresses an action happening at the same time as the main verb, or a quality that applies to a noun. It is also called the *form不确定* (present participle) because it shares the time of the governing verb.

## How to form

Drop the **-ons** ending of the *nous* form and add **-ant**:

| Infinitive | *Nous* form | Participe présent |
|------------|-------------|-------------------|
| **parler** | parlons | **parlant** |
| **finir** | finissons | **finissant** |
| **vendre** | vendons | **vendant** |
| **être** | sommes | **étant** |
| **avoir** | avons | **ayant** |

**Irregular forms:**

| Verb | Participe présent |
|------|------------------|
| *savoir* | sachant |
| *pouvoir* | pouvant |
| *vouloir* | voulant |
| *devoir* | devant |

## When to use

### After prepositions (participal phrases)

When the participle present describes an action by the **same subject** as the main verb, it follows *en*, *pour*, *sans*, etc. The preposition most commonly used is *en*:

*<v data-tense="participe-present">Travaillant</v> dur, il a réussi.* — Working hard, he succeeded.
*Elle est tombée <v data-tense="participe-present">en marchant</v>.* — She fell while walking. *(gérondif — see Le Gérondif)*

### As an adjective substitute (with agreement)

The participe présent can replace a relative clause. It behaves like an adjective and agrees in gender and number with the noun it modifies:

*Les étudiants <v data-tense="participe-present">apprenant</v> vite sont reçus.* — The students who learn quickly are accepted.
*La femme <v data-tense="participe-present">portant</v> une robe bleue est ma sœur.* — The woman wearing a blue dress is my sister.

### After perception verbs (voyage, entendre, regarder)

*Je le vois <v data-tense="participe-present">partir</v>.* — I see him leaving.
*Je l'ai entendue <v data-tense="participe-present">chanter</v>.* — I heard her singing.

## Difference from the présent de l'indicatif

The forms look identical for **-er verbs** (*il parle* = présent, *parlant* = participe présent). But for **-ir** and **-re verbs**, the forms differ:

| Infinitive | Présent (*il*) | Participe présent |
|------------|---------------|-------------------|
| *finir* | il finit | **finissant** |
| *vendre* | il vend | **vendant** |
| *pouvoir* | il peut | **pouvant** |

## Common pitfalls

- The participe présent after *en* (gérondif) is **always invariable** — *en mangeant*, never *en mangeante*.
- When used as an adjective substitute, the participe présent **agrees** with its noun: *une femme portant un chapeau* (feminine singular).
- Do not confuse: *un homme aimant les chats* (participe présent — "a man who loves cats") vs *un homme aimable* (adjective — "a friendly man").
