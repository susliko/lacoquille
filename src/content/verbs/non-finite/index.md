## What are non-finite verb forms?

Non-finite verb forms are verb forms that **do not express a specific subject or time**. Unlike conjugated tenses (e.g., *je parle, il mangeait*), non-finite forms cannot stand alone as the main verb of an independent clause. They depend on a conjugated verb that provides their subject and temporal context.

French has four non-finite forms:

| Form | Ending | Example |
|------|--------|---------|
| **L'infinitif** | -er, -ir, -re | *parler, finir, vendre* |
| **Le participe présent** | -ant | *parlant, finissant, vendant* |
| **Le participe passé** | -é, -i, -u (+ irregulars) | *parlé, fini, vendu* |
| **Le gérondif** | en + -ant | *en parlant* |

## How they relate to tenses

Non-finite forms do not have temporal meaning on their own — their time is supplied by the conjugated verb that governs them:

- *Je veux **partir*** — I want to leave (present tense of *vouloir* → infinitive expresses future-ish action)
- *Il a promis **de partir*** — He promised to leave (past tense → infinitive still expresses future relative to the promise)
- ***Ayant fini** son travail, il est sorti.* — Having finished his work, he left (*ayant fini* is past participle + avoir = anterior to *est sorti*)

## Why they matter

Non-finite forms allow you to chain actions, describe circumstances, and compress ideas that would otherwise require full subordinate clauses:

- *Je l'ai vu **entrer*** — I saw him enter (one clause instead of *je l'ai vu et il est entré*)
- ***Etant fatigué**, il est rentré tôt.* — Being tired, he went home early (participal phrase instead of *parce qu'il était fatigué*)

## The four forms at a glance

| Form | Can be main verb? | Agrees? | Time reference |
|------|------------------|---------|----------------|
| Infinitif | No (depends on modal/preposition) | No | Inherits from governing verb |
| Participe présent | No (adjective-like function) | Yes (as adjective) | Simultaneous with governing verb |
| Participe passé | No (used in compound tenses / as adjective) | Sometimes (avoir) | Anterior or simultaneous |
| Gérondif | No (adverbial function) | No | Simultaneous, with *en* circumstance |
