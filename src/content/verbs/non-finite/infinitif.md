## What is the infinitive

The **infinitif** is the base form of the verb — the dictionary entry. Unlike conjugated forms, it does not express a specific subject or time. Its subject and temporal meaning come from the verb that governs it.

## How to form

| Verb type | Ending | Example |
|-----------|--------|---------|
| **-ER** | -er | *parler* (to speak) |
| **-IR** | -ir | *finir* (to finish) |
| **-RE** | -re | *vendre* (to sell) |
| Irregular | various | *être, avoir, faire, aller, pouvoir, vouloir, devoir* |

## When to use

### After modal verbs

These verbs are followed directly by the infinitive (no preposition):

| Modal verb | Meaning | Example |
|------------|---------|---------|
| **pouvoir** | to be able to / may | *Je peux partir.* — I can leave. |
| **vouloir** | to want | *Elle veut apprendre.* — She wants to learn. |
| **devoir** | must / to have to | *Nous devons finir.* — We must finish. |
| **savoir** | to know how | *Je sais nager.* — I know how to swim. |
| **aimer mieux** | to prefer | *Il aime mieux dormir.* — He prefers to sleep. |
| **faire** | to make / to have (causative) | *Je fais manger les enfants.* — I make the children eat. |

### After prepositions

When a verb follows a preposition, it must be in the infinitive:

**With *pour* (purpose — "in order to"):**
*Il court pour maigrir.* — He runs to lose weight.

**With *avant de* (before doing something):**
*Avant de partir, ferme la porte.* — Before leaving, close the door.

**With *sans* (without doing something):**
*Elle est partie sans dire au revoir.* — She left without saying goodbye.

**With *après* (after doing something):**
*Après avoir mangé, il dort.* — After eating, he sleeps. *(après* + past infinitive *avoir mangé*)

**With *de* (after certain adjectives and verbs):**
*Je suis content de te voir.* — I'm happy to see you.
*Elle a décidé de partir.* — She decided to leave.

### As a noun substitute

The infinitive can function as a noun — it can be the subject or object of a sentence:

*<v data-tense="infinitif">Manger</v> trop est mauvais pour la santé.* — Eating too much is bad for your health.
*J'aime <v data-tense="infinitif">dormir</v>.* — I love to sleep / I love sleeping.

## Impersonal infinitive constructions

With impersonal expressions, the subject of the infinitive is still the subject of the main clause:

*C'est bien de <v data-tense="infinitif">savoir</v> nager.* — It's good to know how to swim.
*Il est important de <v data-tense="infinitif">finir</v> à temps.* — It's important to finish on time.

## Common pitfalls

- ***avant de*** + infinitive vs ***avant que*** + subjunctive: *Avant de partir* (I leave) vs *Avant qu'il parte* (before he leaves — different subjects, requires *que* + subjunctive).
- The infinitive cannot have its own subject — *je veux partir* ✓, but *je veux je parte* ✗. To express "I want **him** to leave," French uses a different construction (*je veux qu'il parte* — with *que* + subjunctive).
