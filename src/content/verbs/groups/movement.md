## Understanding Motion in French

French motion verbs are deceptively simple-seeming but carry subtle semantic distinctions that English speakers must learn to feel intuitively. Unlike English "to go" or "to come," which depend heavily on the speaker's perspective, French motion verbs encode precise information about **direction**, **origin**, and **destination**.

## The Seven Verbs

These seven verbs form the core vocabulary for expressing movement in French:

| Verb | Core Meaning | Direction |
|------|-------------|-----------|
| **aller** | to go | away from speaker |
| **venir** | to come | toward speaker |
| **arriver** | to arrive | endpoint of motion |
| **partir** | to leave | point of departure |
| **entrer** | to enter | motion into something |
| **sortir** | to exit | motion out of something |
| **rester** | to remain | no motion (state) |

## Motion Semantics

### Directional Pairings

French motion verbs work in natural pairs:

- **Aller/Venir**: The aller/venir pair relates to the speaker's position. *Je vais à Paris* (I'm going there, away from you). *Je viens à toi* (I'm coming to you).

- **Entrer/Sortir**: These describe motion through a threshold. *J'entre dans la maison* (I go into the house). *Je sors de la maison* (I go out of the house).

- **Arriver/Partir**: These describe the endpoint or starting point of a journey. *Je pars de Paris* (I leave from Paris). *J'arrive à Paris* (I arrive in Paris).

### The Special Case of *Rester*

*Rester* is unique among these seven verbs because it expresses a **state of remaining** rather than motion. It answers the question "where?" rather than "where to/from?" — *Je reste à la maison* means "I stay at home" or "I remain at home."

## The Periphrastic Constructions

Two important verb phrases work with many of these motion verbs:

- **Être en train de + infinitive**: Expresses ongoing action. *Je suis en train de partir* — I'm in the process of leaving.

- **Venir de + infinitive**: Expresses recent past. *Je viens d'arriver* — I've just arrived.

## Choosing the Right Verb

The choice between these verbs depends on answering specific questions:

1. Is there motion or just a state? → If state, use **rester**
2. Is the motion toward or away from the speaker? → **venir** vs **aller**
3. Is the motion into or out of an enclosed space? → **entrer** vs **sortir**
4. Does the sentence emphasize arrival or departure? → **arriver** vs **partir**
