## What it is

The **passé composé** is the main past tense in spoken and informal written French. It expresses a completed action — one that happened at a specific moment and is now over. It's also the tense you use for a sequence of events that advance the story.

## When to use

- **Single completed past action**: *Elle a téléphoné à midi.* — She called at noon.
- **Sequence of events** (narrative): *Il est entré, a pris son manteau et est reparti.* — He came in, grabbed his coat, and left.
- **Completed action with present relevance**: *J'ai perdu mes clés.* — I've lost my keys (and still don't have them).
- **Action that interrupts an imparfait**: *Je lisais quand il a frappé.* — I was reading when he knocked.
- **Limited number of times**: *Je l'ai vue trois fois.* — I've seen her three times.

## How to form

**Auxiliary (présent) + past participle**

| Auxiliary | Used with |
|-----------|-----------|
| **avoir** | Most verbs |
| **être**  | 17 motion/state intransitives + all reflexive/pronominal verbs |

**Past participle formation:**
- *-er* verbs → **-é** (*parler → parlé*)
- *-ir* verbs → **-i** (*finir → fini*)
- *-re* verbs → **-u** (*vendre → vendu*)
- Irregulars: *être → été*, *avoir → eu*, *faire → fait*, *prendre → pris*, *mettre → mis*, *voir → vu*…

**The être verbs** (DR MRS P VANDERTRAMP): *Devenir, Revenir, Monter, Rester, Sortir, Passer, Venir, Aller, Naître, Descendre, Entrer, Rentrer, Tomber, Retourner, Arriver, Mourir, Partir*.

## Common pitfalls

- **Auxiliary choice** is the first hurdle. When in doubt, ask: is the verb reflexive? → être. Is it on the DR MRS P list? → être. Otherwise → avoir.
- **Agreement with être**: the past participle behaves like an adjective agreeing with the subject. *Il est parti* but *Elle est partie*.
- **Agreement with avoir**: only triggers when a direct object precedes the verb — rare in speech but tested in writing.
