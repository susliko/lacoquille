## What it is

The **subjonctif imparfait** is the past subjunctive used in formal literary French. It appears in 19th-century and classical literature wherever the main clause is in the past and the subjonctif présent would otherwise be used. In modern French — even in formal writing — it is almost entirely replaced by the subjonctif présent.

## When to use

In **literary and classical texts**, when:
- The main clause is in a past tense (passé simple, imparfait, conditionnel)
- The subjunctive is required by the usual triggers
- The subordinate action is simultaneous with or after the main action

**Modern equivalent:** Simply use subjonctif présent in all registers today.

| Literary | Modern equivalent |
|----------|------------------|
| *Il voulait qu'elle vînt.* | *Il voulait qu'elle vienne.* |
| *Bien qu'il fût tard…* | *Bien qu'il soit tard…* |

## How to form

Take the **2nd person singular passé simple** (tu form), drop the final **-s**, and add: *-sse, -sses, -^t, -ssions, -ssiez, -ssent*

| Verb    | Passé simple (tu) | je      | il/elle | nous        |
|---------|------------------|---------|---------|-------------|
| parler  | parlas            | parlasse | parlât | parlassions |
| finir   | finis             | finisse  | finît  | finissions  |
| vendre  | vendis            | vendisse | vendît | vendissions |
| être    | fus               | fusse    | fût    | fussions    |
| avoir   | eus               | eusse    | eût    | eussions    |
| faire   | fis               | fisse    | fît    | fissions    |
| venir   | vins              | vinsse   | vînt   | vinssions   |

> The circumflex on the 3rd person singular (*parlât, finît, fût*) is the key recogniser when reading classical texts.
