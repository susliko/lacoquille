## What it is

The **présent de l'indicatif** is the default tense for anything happening now, habitually, or as a general truth. It covers a wider range than English: *je parle* can mean "I speak," "I am speaking," or "I do speak."

## When to use

- **Current action:** *Elle parle au téléphone.* — She is on the phone.
- **Habitual action:** *Je bois du café le matin.* — I drink coffee in the morning.
- **General truth / permanent state:** *L'eau bout à 100°C.* — Water boils at 100°C.
- **Near future (informal):** *On mange dans cinq minutes.* — We're eating in five minutes.
- **Historical present** (for vivid narration): *Napoléon arrive à Moscou et trouve une ville déserte.* — Napoleon arrives in Moscow and finds a deserted city.
- **After *si* in real conditions:** *Si tu viens, on fera une fête.* — If you come, we'll have a party.

## How to form

**Regular -ER verbs** (*parler*):

| je    | tu     | il/elle | nous    | vous    | ils/elles |
|-------|--------|---------|---------|---------|-----------|
| parle | parles | parle   | parlons | parlez  | parlent   |

Drop the **-er** and add: **-e, -es, -e, -ons, -ez, -ent**.

> Note: *je, tu, il* share the same spoken sound — the silent -e/-es/-e endings.

## Common pitfalls

- *Depuis* + présent = ongoing action that started in the past and continues now. Don't switch to a past tense: *J'habite ici depuis 2010* ✓, not *J'ai habité ici depuis 2010* ✗.
- Stem-changing verbs (-er verbs with spelling shifts in boot forms): *préférer → je préfère* but *nous préférons*.
