## What it is

The **futur simple** expresses actions or states that will occur in the future. It carries a sense of certainty or firm intention, and is required after certain time conjunctions even when English uses the present tense.

## When to use

- **Future action or event**: *Il pleuvra demain.* — It will rain tomorrow.
- **Prediction or supposition**: *Ce sera difficile.* — That will be difficult.
- **Firm intention or promise**: *Je te rappellerai ce soir.* — I'll call you back tonight.
- **Instructions / directions** (official or formal tone): *Vous remplirez le formulaire et le signerez.* — You will fill in the form and sign it.
- **After time conjunctions referring to the future** (*quand, lorsque, dès que, aussitôt que, une fois que, tant que, pendant que*): *Dès qu'il arrivera, nous commencerons.* — As soon as he arrives, we'll start.

## How to form

**Infinitive + futur endings** (for -re verbs, drop the final -e first)

| Person | Ending | *parler* | *finir* | *vendre* |
|--------|--------|----------|---------|---------|
| je     | -ai    | parlerai | finirai | vendrai |
| tu     | -as    | parleras | finiras | vendras |
| il     | -a     | parlera  | finira  | vendra  |
| nous   | -ons   | parlerons| finirons| vendrons|
| vous   | -ez    | parlerez | finirez | vendrez |
| ils    | -ont   | parleront| finiront| vendront|

**Key irregular stems** (endings are always regular):

| Verb    | Stem    |
|---------|---------|
| être    | ser-    |
| avoir   | aur-    |
| aller   | ir-     |
| faire   | fer-    |
| venir   | viendr- |
| voir    | verr-   |
| pouvoir | pourr-  |
| vouloir | voudr-  |
| savoir  | saur-   |

> **Note:** The futur simple stem is identical to the conditionnel présent stem — only the endings differ.

## Common pitfalls

- **After time conjunctions** (*quand* etc.) French mandates futur, not présent. English says "when you arrive" but French requires "quand tu arriveras."
- **Near future** (*aller + infinitif*) is more natural in conversation for imminent events: *Je vais partir* feels more immediate than *Je partirai*. Use futur for scheduled or more distant events.
