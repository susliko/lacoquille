## What it is

The **plus-que-parfait** expresses an action that was already completed at the point when another past action occurred. It is the "past of the past" — always anchored to a reference point in imparfait or passé composé.

## When to use

- **Anterior past**: action A happened before action B, both in the past. *Il avait déjà dormi quand elle est rentrée.* — He had already slept when she came home.
- **Reported speech** (sequence of tenses): a present-tense statement in the original becomes plus-que-parfait when reported in the past. *"J'ai fini" → Il a dit qu'il avait fini.*
- **Si-clauses** (unrealised past conditions): *Si j'avais su, je serais venu.* — If I had known, I would have come. (triggers conditionnel passé in the main clause)
- **Past wish or regret**: *J'aurais voulu qu'il fût là* → more commonly: *J'aurais aimé qu'il soit là.* (The plus-que-parfait structure echoes in regret expressions.)

## How to form

**Imparfait of avoir/être + past participle**

| Person | *parler* (avoir) | *aller* (être) |
|--------|-----------------|----------------|
| je     | avais parlé      | étais allé(e)  |
| tu     | avais parlé      | étais allé(e)  |
| il/elle| avait parlé      | était allé(e)  |
| nous   | avions parlé     | étions allés   |
| vous   | aviez parlé      | étiez allés    |
| ils    | avaient parlé    | étaient allés  |

## Common pitfalls

- **Don't use it in isolation.** *J'avais mangé* by itself is unusual — you almost always need a context like *quand*, *après que*, *avant que*, *déjà*, or a reported speech frame.
- **Si + plus-que-parfait** is a specific construction: the si-clause takes plus-que-parfait, the main clause takes conditionnel passé. Mixing them up (*Si j'aurais su*…) is a very common error.
