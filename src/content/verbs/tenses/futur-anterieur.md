## What it is

The **futur antérieur** expresses a future action that will be completed *before* another future moment. It is the future equivalent of the plus-que-parfait: just as the plus-que-parfait is "past before past," the futur antérieur is "future before future."

## When to use

- **Action completed before a future reference point**: *J'aurai fini avant midi.* — I will have finished before noon.
- **After time conjunctions** (*quand, lorsque, dès que, aussitôt que, une fois que*) when the action must logically be completed first: *Dès qu'elle sera arrivée, nous déjeunerons.*
- **Supposition about a past action from a future perspective**: *Il sera déjà rentré* (= he will probably already be home by then).
- **Expressing a completed consequence**: *Quand tu auras lu ça, tu comprendras tout.*

## How to form

**Futur simple of avoir/être + past participle**

| Person | *parler* (avoir) | *partir* (être)   |
|--------|-----------------|-------------------|
| je     | aurai parlé      | serai parti(e)    |
| tu     | auras parlé      | seras parti(e)    |
| il     | aura parlé       | sera parti        |
| nous   | aurons parlé     | serons partis     |
| vous   | aurez parlé      | serez partis      |
| ils    | auront parlé     | seront partis     |

## Common pitfalls

- **After time conjunctions**, use futur antérieur (not passé composé or plus-que-parfait) to signal that the first action is complete before the second future one starts.
- Same auxiliary rules as passé composé — *être* for motion/state/reflexives, *avoir* for everything else.
