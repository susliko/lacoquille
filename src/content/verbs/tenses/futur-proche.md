## What it is

The **futur proche** (*near future* or *immediate future*) is the most common way to talk about the future in spoken and informal French. Despite its name, it doesn't require the event to be imminent — it's used for anything felt as decided, planned, or personally connected to the present.

> **Note:** The futur proche is a **verbal periphrasis** (a multi-word construction), not a conjugated tense like futur simple. It is formed using the verb *aller* as an auxiliary.

## When to use

- **Imminent or near future**: *Je vais appeler.* — I'm going to call.
- **Planned or decided future**: *On va se marier en juin.* — We're going to get married in June.
- **Predictions with present evidence**: *Regarde ces nuages — il va pleuvoir.* — Look at those clouds — it's going to rain.
- **Informal future in general**: In conversation, futur proche often replaces futur simple for any future event, regardless of how near it is.

**Futur proche vs futur simple:**
Use futur simple for more formal, distant, or hypothetical futures. In speech, futur proche handles almost everything:

| Register | Preference |
|----------|------------|
| Spoken / informal | Futur proche (*Je vais partir*) |
| Written / formal | Futur simple (*Je partirai*) |
| After *quand* (future time clauses) | Futur simple required (*Quand tu arriveras…*) |

## How to form

**Présent of *aller* + infinitif of the main verb**

| Person | *aller* (présent) | + infinitif | Full form |
|--------|------------------|-------------|-----------|
| je     | vais             | parler       | vais parler |
| tu     | vas              | parler       | vas parler  |
| il/elle| va               | parler       | va parler   |
| nous   | allons           | parler       | allons parler |
| vous   | allez            | parler       | allez parler |
| ils    | vont             | parler       | vont parler |

**Negative:** place *ne…pas* around *aller* (not around the infinitif): *Je ne vais pas partir.*

**With a pronoun object:** pronoun goes before the infinitif: *Je vais le faire.* — I'm going to do it.

## In the past

*Aller* in the imparfait + infinitif = *futur dans le passé* (an action that was about to happen):
*Il allait partir quand le téléphone a sonné.* — He was about to leave when the phone rang.
