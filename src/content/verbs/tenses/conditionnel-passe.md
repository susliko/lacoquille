## What it is

The **conditionnel passé** expresses what *would have* happened if conditions had been different. It describes unrealised past situations, regret, reproach, or — like the conditionnel présent — unverified past information.

## When to use

- **Unrealised past condition** (*si + plus-que-parfait* → conditionnel passé): *Si tu avais appelé, je t'aurais aidé.* — If you had called, I would have helped you.
- **Past regret or wish**: *J'aurais voulu être là.* — I would have liked to be there.
- **Reproach or missed obligation**: *Tu aurais dû réfléchir.* — You should have thought about it. *Il aurait pu faire mieux.* — He could have done better.
- **Unverified past information** (journalistic): *Il aurait commis une fraude.* — He is alleged to have committed fraud.
- **Future-in-the-past** (reported speech): *Elle avait promis qu'elle serait arrivée avant midi.* — She had promised she would have arrived by noon.

## How to form

**Conditionnel présent of avoir/être + past participle**

| Person | *parler* (avoir)  | *partir* (être)      |
|--------|------------------|----------------------|
| je     | aurais parlé      | serais parti(e)      |
| tu     | aurais parlé      | serais parti(e)      |
| il     | aurait parlé      | serait parti         |
| nous   | aurions parlé     | serions partis       |
| vous   | auriez parlé      | seriez partis        |
| ils    | auraient parlé    | seraient partis      |

## Common pitfalls

- **Si + conditionnel passé is wrong.** The si-clause *always* takes plus-que-parfait: *Si j'avais su* (✓), never *si j'aurais su* (✗).
- Conditionnel passé is also the standard form for **regret and reproach** without any si-clause: *On aurait pu mieux faire.*
