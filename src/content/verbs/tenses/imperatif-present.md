## What it is

The **impératif présent** is used to give commands, make requests, offer invitations, or give instructions. It is the only French verb form that omits the subject pronoun entirely.

## When to use

- **Direct command**: *Viens ici !* — Come here!
- **Request (polite)**: *Attendez un moment, s'il vous plaît.* — Please wait a moment.
- **Invitation or suggestion** (nous form): *Allons au cinéma.* — Let's go to the cinema.
- **Instructions** (recipes, manuals, signs): *Ajouter le sel. Mélanger.* — Add the salt. Mix.
- **Prohibition** (negative imperative): *Ne fumez pas.* — Do not smoke.

## How to form

The imperative uses the **présent indicatif** forms for tu/nous/vous — without the subject pronoun. One key exception:

**-er verbs and *aller***: drop the final **-s** from the tu form.

| Verb   | tu form  | nous form  | vous form |
|--------|----------|------------|-----------|
| parler | **parle** | parlons   | parlez    |
| finir  | finis     | finissons  | finissez  |
| vendre | vends     | vendons    | vendez    |
| être   | **sois**  | soyons     | soyez     |
| avoir  | **aie**   | ayons      | ayez      |
| aller  | **va**    | allons     | allez     |
| savoir | **sache** | sachons    | sachez    |

**Negative imperative**: *ne* + verb + *pas* (object pronouns go between ne and verb): *Ne le fais pas !*

**Reflexive imperative:** pronoun after verb: *Lève-toi, Levons-nous, Levez-vous*. In negative: *Ne te lève pas*.

## Common pitfalls

- **The missing -s on -er tu forms**: *mange, chante, parle* (no -s) — but *vas-y* and *parles-en* keep the -s for euphony before *y/en*.
- **Object pronoun order** in positive imperatives: *Donne-le-moi* (verb – direct – indirect). In negative: normal pre-verb order: *Ne me le donne pas*.
- **Vous** form can be singular or plural — it's the polite form for one person too.
