## What it is

The **impératif passé** orders someone to have completed an action by a specified point in the future. It is rare — you are more likely to encounter it in formal writing, law, or literary prose than in conversation.

## When to use

- **Command that must be completed by a deadline**: *Ayez rendu vos devoirs avant lundi.* — Have your homework handed in by Monday.
- **Completion before an implied event**: *Aie fini quand je reviendrai.* — Be done when I get back.
- **Formal instructions**: *Ayez pris connaissance du règlement avant de signer.* — Have read the regulations before signing.

In everyday speech, French speakers usually use the impératif présent + a time expression (*Finis ça avant lundi*) or the futur simple instead.

## How to form

**Impératif présent of avoir/être + past participle**

| Person | *parler* (avoir) | *partir* (être)   |
|--------|-----------------|-------------------|
| tu     | aie parlé        | sois parti(e)     |
| nous   | ayons parlé      | soyons partis     |
| vous   | ayez parlé       | soyez partis      |

The imperative passé has only the same three persons as impératif présent (tu / nous / vous) — never je, il, or ils.
